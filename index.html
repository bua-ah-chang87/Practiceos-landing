The Challenge

Regulated professional firms face
a specific AI problem.

The AI market is saturated with tools, vendors, and implementation claims. For advisory practices operating under formal professional obligations, the question is not simply whether AI is useful. It is whether a given tool is appropriate for a specific workflow, within a specific professional context, at an acceptable level of risk.


Guidance that genuinely understands how compliance-bound practices operate — and that carries no commercial interest in any particular platform — is precisely what most principals cannot find at an accessible entry point.

01
Vendor-driven advice dominates the market

Most AI guidance reaching professional firms comes from platform vendors with a commercial interest in adoption. Unconflicted, structured advice is genuinely scarce.

02
High-trust regulated practices require sector knowledge

Generic AI consultants lack working knowledge of financial planning workflows, accounting obligations, legal professional standards, or the specific administrative contexts of compliance-bound firms. Advice without that context is advice without value.

03
Principals lack time to evaluate AI tools on their own

Financial planning, accounting, and legal principals are capacity-constrained. Structured, time-bounded guidance removes a barrier that otherwise keeps most practices in a state of expensive inaction.

04
AI governance is an emerging risk gap

Firms adopting AI tools without structured governance are accumulating risk. ASIC and AFSL obligations increasingly require oversight of technology use in client-facing workflows — a gap many advisory practices have not yet addressed.

What a Snapshot finding
might look like.

Illustrative Example · Financial Planning Practice
Hypothetical Scenario
Consider a four-adviser financial planning practice with three support staff. In this illustration, a Snapshot workflow review identifies approximately six hours per week of repetitive administrative drafting across client correspondence and internal documentation tasks.

The Snapshot maps these workflows in detail, identifies two suitable AI-assisted drafting applications consistent with the firm's existing infrastructure, and provides a conservative time-saving estimate using the firm's own hourly cost assumptions.

The written report outlines specific workflow steps where AI tools could be applied, notes the boundaries of those applications, and recommends the firm seek specialist input before any implementation touching client-facing or compliance-sensitive documentation.
